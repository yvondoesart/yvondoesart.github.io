About

Greetings and salutations! My name is Yvon Lu, and I am a young Vietnamese American artist located in NYC, NY. I’ve been drawing since childhood, but I fell in love with charcoal during a class called New York City as the Muse. The medium was addictive, fun, and encouraged me to push the boundaries of the monochrome. I love pushing values to the extremes and relying on contrast to tell a complete story. My work also often invokes charcoal textures to communicate themes and motifs. I am primarily interested in exploring the uncanny, the unsettling, and the candid, moments that don’t demand attention yet linger in the mind.

Influenced by Gothic literature, speculative anatomy, and classic horror—from Lovecraft to black-and-white monster films like Dracula and Frankenstein—I aim to draw viewers into a world that is familiar yet disquieting. In each piece, I want the textures of the medium itself to be felt: the grit, the smudge, the sudden hard edge. Above all, I hope my work leaves a lingering unease, as if you’ve stumbled across something you weren’t meant to see.
